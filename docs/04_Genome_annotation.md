# Genome Annotation

## Overview

Genome annotation was performed using **Prokka**, which identified protein-coding genes, transfer RNAs (tRNAs), ribosomal RNAs (rRNAs), and functional annotations within the *Colwellia psychrerythraea* genome.

## Annotation Summary

| Feature | Count |
|----------|------:|
| Genome Size | 5,373,180 bp |
| Contigs | 1 |
| Protein Coding Genes (CDS) | 4,507 |
| tRNA Genes | 89 |
| rRNA Genes | 28 |
| tmRNA | 1 |
| Hypothetical Proteins | 2,015 |

Approximately **44.7%** of the predicted proteins were annotated as hypothetical proteins, suggesting that a substantial portion of the genome consists of proteins with unknown or uncharacterized biological functions. This is common in environmental bacteria and highlights opportunities for future functional studies.

# Cold Adaptation Genes

Several genes associated with adaptation to low-temperature environments were identified.

| Gene | Function |
|------|----------|
| cspE | Cold shock protein |
| cspV | Cold shock protein |
| cspD | Cold shock-like protein |

Cold shock proteins act as RNA chaperones that stabilize RNA molecules and assist in transcription and translation during cold stress. Their presence is consistent with the psychrophilic lifestyle of *Colwellia psychrerythraea*.

---

# Molecular Chaperones

The genome contains several molecular chaperones involved in protein folding and stress response.

| Gene | Function |
|------|----------|
| GroES | Co-chaperonin |
| GroEL | Chaperonin |
| DnaK | Heat/Cold stress chaperone |
| ClpB | Protein disaggregation chaperone |

These proteins help maintain protein stability under stressful environmental conditions such as freezing temperatures.

- atpA
- atpB
- atpC
- atpD
- atpE
- atpF
- atpG
- atpH

These genes encode components of the ATP synthase complex responsible for cellular ATP production through oxidative phosphorylation.

# Membrane Transport Systems

Numerous transport-related genes were identified.

Examples include:

- Vitamin B12 transporters (BtuB)
- ABC transporters
- Iron transporters (FeoB)
- Ammonium transporter (AmtB)
- Putrescine transporter (PotE)
- Nicotinamide transporter (PnuC)
- Pyrroloquinoline quinone transporter (PqqU)

These transport systems enable nutrient uptake, ion transport, and adaptation to nutrient-limited marine environments.

# Biological Interpretation

The genome annotation indicates that *Colwellia psychrerythraea* possesses genes involved in:

- Cold adaptation
- Protein quality control
- ATP production
- Membrane transport
- Nutrient acquisition
- Environmental stress response

These findings are consistent with the organism's ability to survive and grow in permanently cold polar marine ecosystems.
