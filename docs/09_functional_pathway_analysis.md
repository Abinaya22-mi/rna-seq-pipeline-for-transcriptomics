# Functional Pathway Analysis

## Objective

The metabolic potential of *Colwellia psychrerythraea* was inferred from genome annotation generated using Prokka and functional annotation using DIAMOND. Because KEGG database retrieval was unavailable, pathway reconstruction was based on identified functional genes.

---

| Protein quality control | 5 chaperones (dnaK, groEL, groES, clpB) | Protein folding during stress |

---

## Biological Interpretation

The genome indicates that *Colwellia psychrerythraea* is highly adapted to cold marine environments. The presence of multiple cold-shock proteins and molecular chaperones suggests mechanisms for maintaining protein stability and RNA function under low-temperature conditions. Numerous transporter proteins indicate strong nutrient acquisition capabilities, while the complete ATP synthase operon supports efficient energy production. The large number of hypothetical proteins highlights opportunities for future functional characterization.

---

## Conclusion

Functional annotation demonstrates that the organism possesses genes involved in energy metabolism, environmental adaptation, transport, and stress response, supporting its survival in polar marine ecosystems.
